# Car Art Rules

Use these rules for every car stage asset.

- Source canvas: PNG, 512x512 px, transparent background after chroma-key removal.
- Production asset: optimized WebP, 512x512 px, transparent background.
- Generation background: flat solid #00ff00 chroma-key only, then remove the key to alpha and convert the final stage art to WebP.
- Camera: fixed front-left 3/4 view, slightly above wheel height, car centered.
- Framing: full vehicle visible, generous transparent padding, no cropping.
- Style: realistic product/game asset render, clean studio lighting, sharp body lines, mild reflections on paint and metal.
- Consistency: all three stages for one car must keep the same silhouette, angle, wheelbase, lighting, scale, and camera distance.
- No legal marks: no brand badges, manufacturer logos, copyrighted decals, exact movie text, license plates, watermarks, UI, or readable text.
- Stage 1: severe barn-find/wreck state. The car must look clearly broken and far from road-ready: heavy dents, missing bumper/trim, cracked or missing glass, exposed wiring, mismatched or missing panels, rusty/dirty dull finish, sagging stance, worn or flat tires. Keep the core silhouette recognizable.
- Stage 2: partial restoration / rolling project, not fully complete. The car is assembled enough to read as the icon, but still unfinished: primer or mismatched panels, incomplete trim, temporary wheels, visible panel gaps, missing small details, unpolished paint/metal, workshop-ready rather than showroom-ready.
- Stage 3: respectful premium restomod/show build. It must be clearly more advanced than stage 2: flawless paint/metal, lowered confident stance, high-quality modern wheels, tasteful period-compatible aero lip or splitter, upgraded lights, cleaner panel fit, subtle performance vents/cooling details, polished museum/show finish. Do not use garish body kits, giant fantasy wings, neon, sponsor decals, flames, stickers, text, logos, or anything that mocks the original icon.
